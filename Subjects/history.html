📜 History of Filipinos

Explore the rich and fascinating history of the Filipino people.

📌 Interesting Facts About Filipino History

Pre-Colonial Period (Before 1565)

• Literature was oral and passed down through generations.
• Forms include epics, riddles, chants, and songs.
• Early Filipino societies had rich oral traditions, such as the epic of Biag ni Lam-ang and Hudhud.

Spanish Colonial Period (1565-1898)

• Literature became heavily influenced by Christianity and Spanish culture.
• The Pasyon (religious epic) and corridos (romantic ballads) were widely popular.
• The rise of nationalist literature, with works like Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo by José Rizal.

American Colonial Period (1898-1946)

• English became the medium of instruction, introducing new literary forms.
• Filipino writers used English to express nationalist sentiments.
• Writers like Carlos P. Romulo, Nick Joaquin, and Francisco Balagtas made significant contributions.

Japanese Occupation (1942-1945)

• Literature reflected the struggle during World War II.
• Writers used Tagalog and regional languages for resistance.
• Haiku poetry became popular due to Japanese influence.

Post-War Period (1946-1970s)

• Literature focused on modernism and social realism.
• Themes of independence, identity, and post-colonial struggles were explored.

Martial Law Era (1972-1981)

• The rise of underground literature opposing dictatorship.
• Writers used allegories, symbolism, and coded language.
• Notable writers include Jose F. Lacaba, Lualhati Bautista, and NVM Gonzalez.

Contemporary Period (1980s-present)

• Filipino literature is now diverse, covering globalization and identity.
• The rise of digital literature and social media writings.
• Writers focus on personal stories, family relationships, and social commentary.

📌 José Rizal: Background & Facts

• Full Name: José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonzo Realonda
• Born: June 19, 1861 – Calamba, Laguna
• Parents: Francisco Mercado & Teodora Alonzo
• Siblings: 10 (seventh child)

• Education:
  - Ateneo Municipal de Manila – Bachelor of Arts
  - University of Santo Tomas – Medicine (unfinished)
  - Universidad Central de Madrid – Medicine & Philosophy

• Languages: Fluent in 22 languages
• Notable Works: Noli Me Tangere & El Filibusterismo
• Advocacy: Peaceful reform, education, and equality
• Exile: Dapitan (1892–1896)
• Death: December 30, 1896 – Executed in Bagumbayan (now Luneta)
• Legacy: Inspired the Philippine Revolution & declared national hero
